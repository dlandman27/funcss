The Last Time

some moments pass without a goodbye
↓ scroll ↓
There was a last time your parents carried you — and neither of you knew it.
No fanfare. No photograph. Just an ordinary afternoon, and then it was over.
There's a last time you'll hear your favorite song for the very first time.
That version of it — raw and brand new — exists only once.
You've already had your last first day of school.
The nerves, the new backpack smell, the wondering who you'd sit with — behind you now.
There was a last time you played outside until the streetlights came on.
You went inside, and that chapter closed without a title page.
There's a last time you'll run into an old friend by accident.
Some people drift out of your orbit so slowly you don't notice them go.
There was a last time you believed in something with your whole heart, before doubt arrived.
The certainty was a gift you didn't know you were holding.
There's a last time your whole family will be in the same room together.
You've already laughed at a joke from someone who made you laugh harder than anyone.
Some of those laughs echo.
There was a last time you rode a bike with no particular destination.
Just wind, and legs, and everywhere.
There's a last time you'll feel the specific joy of a snow day — the one where the whole world cancels.
There was a last time someone tucked you in.
You probably just thought it was Tuesday.
There's a last summer. Not the season — the feeling. The one where time stretched.
Some people have already had theirs.
There was a last time you called someone for no reason at all, just to talk.
Phones got busy. Schedules hardened. The habit quietly disappeared.
There's a last time you'll be surprised by a plot twist.
Patterns become recognizable. The world grows a little more legible.
You've had a last truly dreamless sleep — the kind where the night disappears completely.
There was a last time a stranger's small kindness made your whole day.
You might not remember it. It happened anyway.
There's a last time you'll be genuinely, completely bored.
Attention is a resource now. Empty time has become a luxury.
There was a last time you ran just for the joy of running.
Not for a bus, not for fitness. Just because your legs wanted to.
There's a last time someone will look at you the way only they do.
There was a last time you were completely in the present moment — no past pulling, no future worrying.
Probably when you were very young. Probably on a perfectly ordinary day.
But there are still so many firsts.
You haven't had your last one yet.
